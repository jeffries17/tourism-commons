Market-Product Alignment
Data & Decision-Making
Interpreting Market Signals

Operator with American clients wants to expand to Europe but only sells 5-day packages

Europeans typically have 3-4 weeks vacation vs. Americans' 10 days. Offering 7-10 day packages = better market fit

Website offers "something for everyone" — cultural tours, adventure, nature, wellness — but gets no responses from agencies

Agencies need clear specialization. Focus on one niche = become the go-to expert buyers can easily position

Met 10 tour operators at a trade fair, exchanged cards, but didn't know what to share or how to follow up, so didn't

Simple contact tracking system + follow-up schedule = turn business cards into actual business relationships

Checks website traffic regularly but not what brings visitors (search terms, referral sources, which countries)

Track keywords & sources = understand which markets are actively searching and what they're looking for

Travelers from a market say "great experience!" but buyers from that market say "quoted prices too high"

Different audiences, different concerns. Buyers need clear margins & competitive positioning, not just tourist satisfaction

Multiple travelers mention the same concern (unclear pickup, confusing booking process) but it's never documented

Systematic feedback tracking = spot recurring issues quickly, fix problems before they cost bookings

Discovery-Led Design

Critical Questions We Need to Explore Together

👥 Participant Readiness

  • What is their current experience with technology and data?
  • What software/tools are they already using?
  • What are their expectations for the training?

🤖 AI Scope & Purpose

  • Content creation vs data analysis vs decision support?
  • How do we keep the human element central?
  • Start AI discussion in online module or in-person?

📊 Data Readiness

  • What data do participants currently have access to?
  • What data could they create? (e.g., sentiment analysis of reviews)
  • How can we make data collection practical and sustainable?

🎓 Learning Environment

  • How do we adapt to different experience levels?
  • What examples/case studies resonate with Colombian context?
  • How to balance theory with hands-on practice?

Our Approach

A complete cycle: Analyze → Structure → Test → Adjust

Positioning & Digital Perception

Assess how operators currently appear to European buyers and where market-product gaps exist

Information Systems Setup

Introduce simple tools for tracking buyer contacts, conversations, and feedback systematically

Real Market Testing

Virtual B2B meetings with European tour operators and agencies to validate positioning and gather direct feedback

Feedback Analysis & Strategy

Structured analysis of buyer responses to identify patterns and define next steps for market development

Why Discovery-Led Design

Technology training fails when it's disconnected from participants' reality. Discovery ensures we build training that meets participants where they are and takes them where they need to go — not where a generic curriculum assumes they should be.

📋 Phase 0: Preparation & Diagnostic

Understanding where operators start to adapt training content

🔍Diagnostic Process

  • Market positioning assessment
  • Current digital presence analysis
  • Existing tools & systems inventory
  • International market experience level

👥Group Formation

  • Segment by niche and target markets
  • Match operators with European buyers
  • Prepare customized B2B agendas
  • Identify shared challenges by segment

Sample Diagnostic Questions:

• What is your primary international target market?

• What digital tools do you currently use to manage buyer contacts?

• Have you participated in international B2B meetings before?

• How do you currently track and follow up with international buyers?

💻 Module 1: Online Training

April 27–28 · 9:00 Medellín / 16:00 Paris · 2 sessions (2 hours each)

🌿Session 1 (Apr 27): Responsible Tourism & the European Market

  • What European buyers look for in responsible & sustainable tourism products
  • Responsible tourism market alignment self-assessment
  • Translating responsible practices into commercial value propositions
  • How the responsible tourism segment is growing and segmenting in Europe
  • Tourism 4.0: sustainability as a market advantage

* Content adapted based on participant experience level and market focus

🔍Session 2 (Apr 28): Digital Presence & Responsible Product Visibility

  • How does your responsible tourism offer appear online? Evaluation through multiple lenses:
  • — The buyer lens: what a European tour operator sees when they look you up
  • — The sustainability lens: how your credentials, certifications, and impact claims read
  • — The traveler lens: reviews, storytelling, and platform presence
  • — The competitive lens: how you compare within your responsible tourism niche
  • Digital tools for responsible tourism promotion and data-driven positioning

* Participants receive a personalized digital presence snapshot ahead of the in-person module

Deliverables: Responsible tourism market alignment tool, personalized digital presence assessment, practical guides on buyer relationship management

📍 Module 2: In-Person Training (Medellín)

May 25–26, Medellín · 2 intensive days: pitch preparation + real market testing

Day 1: Positioning & Pitch

Focus:

Preparing participants to pitch their responsible tourism product to European buyers

  • Responsible tourism niche workshop — who is your European buyer?
  • Communicating your responsible practices as a commercial differentiator
  • Adapting your message for different buyer profiles
  • 2-minute pitch practice with peer feedback
  • Materials and presentation refinement

Day 2: Pitch Day

Morning:

  • B2B pitch sessions with international buyers online
  • 10 minutes each with structured feedback

Afternoon:

  • In-person meetings with ~10 French businesses
  • Afternoon recap session

Note: Activities are deliberately general at this stage. More specific workshops can be developed based on participant assessment and digital maturity levels to meet operators where they are.

June 15 · 9:00 Medellín / 16:00 Paris — Turning market feedback into relationships and product development

🗺️ Where We Go From Here

Strategic questions to align implementation with your goals

👥Who Participates?

20 participants — but who exactly?

  • Tour operators only? Or include accommodations, DMCs, public officials?
  • What's their current business stage? (Established vs emerging)
  • Single cohort or multiple focused groups?

This shapes the entire program design and depth of impact.

🤝European Buyer Involvement

How do we activate market connections?

  • Can we access Embassy/Institut Français buyer networks?
  • Which European markets to prioritize? (France, Germany, UK, Netherlands?)
  • Are there existing partnerships with tour operators we can leverage?
  • What's the timeline for coordinating B2B meetings?

📅Implementation Timeline

Working backwards from May 25–26, 2026 in-person training:

  • When can we finalize participant selection?
  • When do discovery assessments need to happen?
  • How much lead time for buyer coordination and B2B setup?
  • When should online module launch?

🎯Logistical Considerations

Practical elements that shape program delivery:

  • Training materials: Spanish only or multilingual?
  • Venue requirements for in-person sessions in Medellín
  • Platform for online module delivery and participant engagement
  • Post-training support structure and follow-up plan
